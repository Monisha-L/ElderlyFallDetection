About Us

The prevalence of falls among the elderly population poses a significant public health concern, leading to injuries, hospitalizations, and even fatalities.

  • About one in three adults aged 65 and older experience a fall each year
  • According to the World Health Organization (WHO), falls are the second leading cause of accidental or unintentional injury deaths worldwide.

In this context, the development of effective fall detection and prevention strategies is paramount to improving the quality of life and safety of the elderly population. The aging population worldwide has led to a growing concern regarding the health and well-being of older adults, particularly in relation to falls.

Why is this solution a need of the hour ?

Existing solutions for elderly fall detection and prevention encompass a range of technological innovations, behavioral interventions, and environmental modifications.

  • 01 Advancements in wearable devices?

    Research has explored various fall detection systems using sensor technology. Wearable devices, such as accelerometers and gyroscopes, are widely used for fall detection. These devices monitor the user's movement patterns and can detect sudden changes in acceleration or orientation, which may indicate a fall.

  • 02 Usage of Ambient Sensors?

    Ambient sensors, such as pressure mats, infrared sensors, and acoustic sensors, are utilized to detect falls in indoor environments. These sensors are installed in strategic locations and can detect changes in pressure, motion, or sound patterns associated with falls.

  • 03 Growth of Vision Based Techniques?

    Vision-based techniques employ cameras and computer vision algorithms to detect falls by analyzing the person's posture, behavior, and movement patterns. These techniques can be implemented using traditional cameras or depth sensors, such as Microsoft Kinect. Advances in machine learning techniques have facilitated the development of predictive models for fall detection. By analyzing sensor data patterns and movement trajectories, machine learning algorithms can distinguish between normal activities and fall events with high accuracy.
